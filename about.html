My Story

The Path Here

I grew up checking boxes. Affluent Baltimore suburb, prestigious private school, varsity lacrosse, the whole trajectory already mapped out. It looked good from the outside. But inside, I was restless—searching for something I couldn’t name, increasingly aware that the path laid out in front of me wasn’t mine.

I found my way into literature. Hesse, Dostoyevsky, Kerouac, Burroughs, Thompson. These writers carried the questions I was carrying. That helped. But books alone weren’t enough.

My first serious encounter with psychedelics changed everything. Not metaphorically—in the most literal sense. The experience didn’t hand me answers. It dissolved the questions I’d been asking and replaced them with better ones. Over the years that followed, I went deep into that world. I developed genuine expertise in substances, in set and setting, in the delicate work of sitting with someone through an experience and helping them make sense of it afterward. Long before integration became a buzzword, I was doing it informally—guiding journeys, running microdosing programs, and watching what happened when people were given the right conditions to actually meet themselves.

Then the DEA caught up with me. I spent time in prison and on probation. That’s the part of this story most people in this space leave out or soften into something more palatable. I’m not interested in doing that. The legal system treated what I was doing as a crime. I treated it as a calling. Both of those things can be true at the same time. And living inside that contradiction taught me more about freedom, authority, and the gap between law and wisdom than anything else I’ve experienced.

I stepped back for a few years to satisfy my legal obligations and rebuild. During that time I kept writing, kept reading, kept thinking. The culture was catching up to everything I’d known firsthand for years—clinical trials, therapeutic frameworks, the slow legitimization of substances that had always worked. When I came back to this work, it was with clearer eyes and a different structure. The Psilosopher is the result of that return. Not a retreat from what I’d learned, but a channel for it that can actually reach people without putting anyone at risk.

I’ve had hundreds of profound experiences myself. I’ve sat with people through theirs. I understand the literature, the philosophy, the neuroscience, and the poetry of expanded consciousness. I also understand what it feels like to go too far, to lose the thread, to need someone to help you find your way back. That’s what I’m here for.

My Purpose

The Mission

To provide honest, grounded, experience-backed education and integration support for people who are serious about using psychedelics as tools for genuine growth.

Not escape. Not entertainment. The difficult and rewarding work of becoming more fully yourself.

What I Stand For

Core Values

Honesty Over Comfort

I’m not going to tell you what you want to hear. The psychedelic space has plenty of people selling transcendence. I’m more interested in the truth, even when it’s complicated or unflattering.

Experience Over Theory

Everything here comes from direct, lived engagement with these substances and with the people who use them. I draw on science and philosophy and indigenous wisdom, but the foundation is firsthand knowledge.

Integration Is the Work

The journey is an hour or a day. Integration is the rest of your life. That’s where the real transformation either happens or doesn’t, and that’s where I focus.

Balance & Discipline

I don’t believe in fear as a governing principle. I don’t believe in binary thinking. I believe in balance, discipline, and your right to explore your own consciousness on your own terms.

The Guide

Who I Am

Deep Experience, Not Just Theory

I’ve had hundreds of profound experiences myself. I’ve sat with people through theirs. I understand the literature, the philosophy, the neuroscience, and the poetry of expanded consciousness. I also understand what it feels like to go too far, to lose the thread, to need someone to help you find your way back.

Grounded in What Works

Years of hands-on experience with set and setting. Understanding how microdosing actually affects people. Knowing the difference between a spiritual experience and psychological integration. These aren’t theoretical concepts for me—they’re lived knowledge, hard-won and tested against real human complexity.

The people who have gone furthest into the dark have the most useful things to say to those who are heading there. If that sounds like your kind of guide, you’re in the right place.
